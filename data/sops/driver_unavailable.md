# SOP-03: Driver Unavailable

## Purpose
Procedure when a rostered driver cannot take or continue an assigned trip (illness, hours-of-service limit, no-show).

## Steps
1. The driver (or depot supervisor) reports unavailability to dispatch as early as possible.
2. Dispatch checks the roster for a **standby** driver whose remaining shift covers the full trip duration.
3. If a standby driver is available, reassign the trip and confirm acknowledgement from the new driver.
4. If none is available, check whether a later trip's driver can be swapped without breaking hours-of-service rules.
5. As a last resort, cancel the lowest-priority affected trip and notify passengers per SOP-02.

## Hours-of-service guardrails
- A driver must not be assigned if the trip would extend their duty beyond the legal daily limit.
- Minimum 30-minute break after 4.5 hours of continuous driving.

## Approval rules
- Standby reassignment: dispatcher may approve.
- Trip cancellation or duty-limit exception requests: operations manager approval required; exceptions must be documented in the incident record.
